Model: Sim2

Description

Model code (ver. 1.0)

This code is a set of equations for an agent-based model, specifically tailored for simulating market dynamics with an emphasis on the role of human capital in competitiveness and technological innovation. Here's a brief description of the various components:

Market Share Dynamics:

z represents the market share of a firm which is updated by a modified replicator dynamics equation. The update takes into account the firm's competitiveness (E), average market competitiveness (AvE), and the influence of human capital accumulation (H) on the firm's adjusted competitiveness.

Competitiveness and Price Setting:

AvE calculates the average competitiveness across firms.
E defines a firm’s competitiveness as inversely proportional to its price (p).
p sets the price based on a markup (mu) over wages (w), adjusted by productivity (A).
Productivity and Technological Level:

A describes a firm's productivity, which evolves based on investment (I), the sum of investments (SumI), previous productivity, and a factor of human capital (H).
a models the technological level of a firm, which may improve depending on the success of R&D efforts, influenced by human capital.
Innovation and Technological Progress:

I defines the investment of a firm, which is the minimum of a fraction (iota) of sales (Y) or profit (Pi).
epsilon and sigma are used to model the potential innovation jump and its volatility, where epsilon follows a normal distribution with standard deviation sigma.
sigma varies depending on whether an innovation (Inn) occurs and is based on the difference between the average technological level in the market (Ava) and the firm's technological level.
Financial Equations:

Y computes sales as the product of market share, and total demand.
Pi calculates the profit of the firm, considering wages, productivity (enhanced by human capital), and sales.
R defines the R&D investment decision based on various factors including wages, a parameter (rho), sales, profit, and investment.
Human Capital Dynamics:

H models human capital accumulation, considering depreciation (delta) and education investments (Ed).
Ed decides on education investment as a fraction (tau) of profit.
